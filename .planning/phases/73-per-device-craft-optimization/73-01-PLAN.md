<objective>
Add genre-aware priority scoring to `getEffectPriority()` in chain-rules.ts so that COMBO-03 truncation respects genre intent. An ambient preset on Pod Go should keep reverb + delay + modulation and drop drive, not the reverse.

Purpose: CRAFT-02 (Pod Go genre-intelligent prioritization) and CRAFT-04 (craft encoded in code, not just prompts). Currently, truncation uses a generic slot-based priority that always favors compressor > drive > delay > reverb > modulation, regardless of genre. This produces musically wrong results for ambient, blues, and jazz presets on budget devices.

Output: Updated `chain-rules.ts` with `GENRE_SLOT_PRIORITY` lookup table, `matchGenreKey()` helper, genre-aware `getEffectPriority()`, and `intent.genreHint` threaded to the COMBO-03 truncation call. Full TDD test coverage.
</objective>
